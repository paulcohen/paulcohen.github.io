Petra

The final product

A belaying, rapelling and ascending device with an innovative safety feature


2.009 is the capstone course for Mechanical Engineering Seniors at MIT, and follows the complete process of developing a product up to an alpha prototype, on a budget of $6500. I was one of the two team leaders responsible for coordinating our team of 22, as well as being a technical lead for design and fabrication of the device.

Starting with the ideation phase, my team and I researched and evaluated hundreds of different ideas, before narrowing down to six - for which we created Sketch Models and Mockups - before settling on Petra: an all-in-one belaying, rappelling, and ascending device with an automatic fall prevention feature for cavers, canyoneers, and climbers.

I was a technical lead for the project, as well as being one of two team leaders responsible for coordinating our team of 22. Skills I used during the project include top-down CAD modelling, CAM, practical mill and lathe work, rapid prototyping, technical drawing, chairing meetings, presentation skills, and project management.
